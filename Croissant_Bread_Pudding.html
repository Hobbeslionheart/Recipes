Croissant Bread Pudding

A rich, custardy dessert that gives new life to stale croissants. Soft inside, golden on top, and endlessly adaptable with chocolate, fruit, or nuts.

Author: Carrie Robarge

Yield: 6–8 servings

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 5–6 stale croissants (medium or large)
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • Butter for greasing dish
  • Optional: ½ cup chocolate chips or berries, ¼ cup sliced almonds or pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9×9-inch baking dish.
  2. Tear croissants into large bite-sized pieces and arrange in the dish.
  3. Whisk together eggs, milk, cream, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Pour custard evenly over croissants, pressing gently to soak. Let sit 10–15 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle optional toppings (chocolate, fruit, or nuts) if desired.
  6. Bake 35–45 minutes, until golden and just set in the center.
  7. Serve warm with powdered sugar, caramel, or ice cream.

Recipe Variations

  • Almond Croissant Pudding: Replace vanilla with 1 tsp almond extract and top with sliced almonds.
  • Chocolate Croissant Pudding: Use chocolate croissants and add 2 Tbsp cocoa powder to the custard.
  • Berry Croissant Pudding: Add 1 cup mixed berries and zest of 1 lemon to the custard.
