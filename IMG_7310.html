Cream of Chicken Soup with Gnocchi Dumplings

Author: Bob Warden
Recipe category: Soups
Serves: Six
Temperature: High
Prep time: 10 mins, Cook time: 6 mins

This cream of chicken soup recipe takes a totally original and delicious turn with the addition of Italian gnocchi dumplings. Now available in almost every grocery store's dry pasta aisle, gnocchi are small potato dumplings that cook up as large and satisfying as a traditional dumpling without all the work!

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons butter or margarine
  • 1 pound chicken tenders, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons flour
  • 2 stalks celery, diced small
  • 4 teaspoons chicken base (see page: 12) mixed into 4 cups water
  • 16 ounces dry gnocchi dumplings (sold in pasta aisle)
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme or 1 teaspoon dried
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon parsley flakes
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream (may use half and half)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat butter
    Heat butter in pressure cooker on high or "brown" until melted.
  2. Coat and sauté chicken
    Coat chicken thigh cubes with the flour on all sides before adding them and the celery to the cooker, sautéing for 2–3 minutes until chicken is lightly browned.
  3. Cover with remaining ingredients
    Cover with remaining ingredients, except for heavy cream.
  4. Lock lid and cook
    Securely lock on the pressure cooker's lid, set the cooker to high and cook for 6 minutes.
  5. Release pressure and finish
    Perform a quick release to release the cooker's pressure. Safely remove lid and slowly stir in the heavy cream. Salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately.

Tip

For firmer, more pasta-like gnocchi, add them with the heavy cream after the pressure cooking process. Switch the cooker to high or "brown" with lid off and boil for 3 to 4 minutes until gnocchi begin to float.
